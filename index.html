Treatment & Procedures

Our experienced doctors and surgeons at Artemis Hospital, who specialize in different areas of heart care, diagnose and treat all kinds of heart diseases.

Coronary Artery Bypass Surgery

A surgical procedure to restore normal blood flow to an obstructed coronary artery.

Congenital Heart Surgery (ASD/VSD Surgery)

Surgical correction of heart defects that are present at birth affecting the structure of the heart.

Minimally Invasive Surgery

Advanced surgical techniques that require smaller incisions resulting in less pain and faster recovery.

Valve Repair

Procedures to repair or replace damaged heart valves to restore proper blood flow through the heart.

Pacemaker

Implantation of electronic devices that help regulate the heartbeat for patients with arrhythmias.

Heart Attack

Emergency treatment for acute myocardial infarction, including thrombolysis and primary angioplasty.

Common warning signs include chest pain or discomfort, shortness of breath, heart palpitations, weakness, dizziness, and unusual fatigue. Some patients may also experience pain or numbness in the arms, neck, jaw, or back. If you're experiencing any of these symptoms, especially chest pain, seek medical attention immediately.

Bring a list of all medications you're currently taking, including over-the-counter drugs and supplements. Compile your medical records, including previous test results. Write down any symptoms you've been experiencing, when they occur, and what makes them better or worse. Prepare questions for your doctor about your condition. Lastly, bring your insurance information and a form of identification.

Recovery times vary depending on the type of procedure and individual factors. Generally, hospital stays range from 3-7 days for traditional open-heart surgeries, while minimally invasive procedures may require just 1-3 days. Full recovery at home takes approximately 6-12 weeks for major surgeries. Our cardiac rehabilitation program will provide personalized guidance throughout your recovery journey.

Adopting heart-healthy lifestyle choices significantly reduces risk. These include maintaining a balanced diet rich in fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and lean proteins; regular physical activity (aim for at least 150 minutes per week); maintaining a healthy weight; quitting smoking; limiting alcohol consumption; managing stress; and getting regular check-ups to monitor blood pressure, cholesterol, and blood sugar levels.
